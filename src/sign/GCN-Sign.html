Prohibited Inducements (Fla. Stat. §§ 489.147 & 626.854)
It is unlawful for any contractor offering residential roofing services to compensate, rebate, or absorb your insurance deductible; to offer services in exchange for letting an insurance claim be filed; or to interpret your policy. Customer is responsible for the deductible. GCN is NOT a licensed Public Adjuster and shall not adjust, negotiate, or settle any claim on your behalf.
Insurance Fraud Warning
Any person who knowingly, with intent to defraud, prepares or presents a proof of loss or repair estimate containing false, incomplete, or misleading information commits a felony of the third degree under Fla. Stat. §§ 775.082, 775.083, 775.084.
Right to Cancel
You may cancel this transaction without penalty within three (3) business days of signing. If your insurance carrier denies your claim in whole or in part, you may also cancel within thirty (30) days of the later of (a) the date GCN issues you a written estimate or (b) the date of denial — without penalty (Fla. Stat. § 489.147(3)). A separate Notice of Cancellation form is provided.
Recovery Fund & Construction Lien Law
Florida Homeowners' Construction Recovery Fund: Up to a limited amount, payment may be available if you lose money on a project performed under contract from violations by a licensed contractor (FCILB: 850-487-1395). Florida Construction Lien Law (Fla. Stat. §§ 713.001–713.37): Those who work on your property or supply materials and aren't paid in full have lien rights, even if you've paid your contractor in full. Full text and details in the Legal Addendum (QR code below).
